  • Staging Lane
    • -Lanes leading up the water box, where racer prepare to run down the dragstrip.

  • Water Box
    • - Area of concrete roughly 50 feet behind the starting line that is sprayed with water to help with burnouts.

  • Burnout
    • -The process of spinning the tires to remove debris, and to heat up the tire for better traction down the track.

  • Christmas Tree
    • - No, not the thing you put in your front room during Christmas. This is a pole that has two small amber “staging” lights. The top amber is the pre-stage and the second is the stage light. The next three larger yellow lights are the “timing” lights. Depending on the kind of tree will depend on how quick the light up. The green “go” light. And the final light on the tree is the red “you left to early” light.

  • Sportsman Tree
    • -This indicates the three yellow “timing” bulbs on the Christmas tree will come on in sequence .500 seconds apart.

  • Pro Tree
    • - This indicates that all three “timing” lights will illuminate at once. Then .400 to .500 seconds later the green light will illuminate.

  • 1/4 Mile
    • - This is the most common type of drag racing. Each racer will line up, side-by-side and race for 1320 feet or ¼ of a mile. The first person to cross the finish line wins the race.

  • 1/8th Mile
    • - Same as the ¼ mile just half the distance.

  • Roll Racing
    • - Competitors will start back about 1000 feet back from the Christmas tree and speed up to the predetermined speed. Once that speed is reached the Christmas tree with go green and the two will race to the finish line.

  • Naturally Aspirated
    • -A car having no form of power adder.

  • Turbocharger
    • -Gas driven turbine that adds compressed air to engine.

  • Centrifugal Supercharger
    • -Crankshaft driven turbine that adds compressed air to the engine.

  • Twin Screw Blower
    • -Supercharger that uses two screw shaped impellers to feed an engine compressed air.

  • Roots Blower
    • -Supercharger that uses two meshing lobes to feed an engine compressed air.

  • Nitrous
    • -Nitrous Oxide, a oxygen additive that when combined with extra fuel creates extra horsepower.

  • Street Racing
    • - Illegal racing on public roads, this can be either drag racing or roll racing.

  • Money Shift
    • -When you shift into a lower gear when trying to shift up a gear for example going 1-2-1, instead of 1-2-3.
